Akara



Description

Akara, also known as bean cake, is a popular West African fritter made from black-eyed peas. It is a popular breakfast dish in Nigeria and other West African countries, but can also be enjoyed as a snack or appetizer at any time of day. Akara is made by blending black-eyed peas with onions, peppers, and spices until smooth. The batter is then deep-fried until golden brown and crispy on the outside and fluffy and soft on the inside. Akara is typically served with a variety of dipping sauces, such as pepper sauce, ketchup, or mustard. Akara is a good source of protein and fiber, and is also low in fat and calories. It is a popular dish among vegetarians and vegans, and is also a good option for people with dietary restrictions.

Ingredients

The ingredients for making Akara are:

  • 2 cups black-eyed peas
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1 habanero pepper, chopped (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Steps

  1. Soak black-eyed peas in water overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  2. Drain and rinse the black-eyed peas.
  3. In a food processor or blender, combine the black-eyed peas, onions, peppers, and spices. Blend until smooth.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or pot over medium heat.
  5. Wet your hands with water and scoop the akara batter into the hot oil. Fry for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Drain the akara on paper towels and serve immediately.
  7. Enjoy!
